# Thailand-Cambodia Border War (2025-2026)

**Status**: ACTIVE CONFLICT  
**Start Date**: November 2025  
**Location**: Preah Vihear Temple border region  
**Casualties**: 23+ deaths (as of March 6, 2026)  
**Displaced**: ~700,000 people  

## Executive Summary

Renewed armed conflict between Thailand and Cambodia over disputed territory near the Preah Vihear Temple has escalated into the worst border clash in decades. The conflict has displaced approximately 700,000 people and killed at least 23 as of early March 2026.

## Timeline

### November 2025
- Initial border skirmishes near Preah Vihear Temple
- Both nations deploy military forces to contested areas
- First civilian casualties reported

### December 2025 - February 2026
- Escalation of military engagements
- Mass displacement begins as border villages evacuated
- International Crisis Group lists as "Conflict to Watch in 2026"

### March 2026 (Current)
- 700,000 displaced persons documented
- 23+ confirmed deaths
- No ceasefire agreement reached
- Regional refugee crisis emerging

## Root Causes

1. **Territorial Disputes**: Long-standing disagreements over Preah Vihear Temple area demarcation
2. **Nationalist Politics**: Domestic political pressures in both countries
3. **Resource Competition**: Access to potential oil/gas reserves in contested areas
4. **Historical Grievances**: Unresolved colonial-era border definitions

## Humanitarian Impact

### Displacement
- **700,000 people** displaced (ACLED data, March 2026)
- Majority from rural border communities
- Refugee camps overcrowded and under-resourced
- Cross-border displacement into Laos reported

### Casualties
- 23+ confirmed deaths (likely undercount)
- Civilians comprise majority of casualties
- Landmine injuries increasing

### Economic
- Border trade halted
- Agricultural disruption in conflict zones
- Tourism collapse in temple regions

## Geopolitical Context

### Regional Powers
- **ASEAN**: Attempting mediation, limited success
- **China**: Economic ties to both nations, unofficial support to Cambodia
- **US**: Security partnership with Thailand complicates neutrality

## Systemic Risks

1. **Regional Destabilization**: Laos, Vietnam drawn into refugee crisis
2. **ASEAN Credibility**: Failure to mediate threatens organizational legitimacy
3. **Great Power Rivalry**: US-China competition exploiting conflict
4. **Precedent Setting**: Emboldens other territorial disputes in SE Asia
